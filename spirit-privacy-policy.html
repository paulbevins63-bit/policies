Privacy Policy — Kentucky Spiritual Guide

Last updated: 14 July 2026 · Kentucky Bootleggers Software Inc. · Contact: Paulie.n.SD@protonmail.com

The Kentucky Spiritual Guide ("the app") is a spiritual and recovery-support utility from Kentucky Bootleggers Software Inc. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with your information. The short version: the app was built to keep your information on your own phone.

What we collect and send to us: nothing

The app has no user accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no tracking. We (the developer) never receive your personal information. There is no server that belongs to us. We cannot see your journal, your profile, your prayers, your location, or anything else you enter.

What stays on your phone

Everything you enter is stored locally on your device and is not transmitted to us:

  • Your journal entries and notes.
  • Your personal profile (preferred name, location text, phone, emergency contacts, recovery milestones such as a clean date or birthday, and the optional "how to talk with me" preferences).
  • Bookmarks, reading history, and any facts the on-device companion learns.

This data lives in the app's private storage. Uninstalling the app removes it.

When the app connects to the internet

The app is useful offline, but a few features reach the internet. In each case, only what is described below is sent, and it is never sent to us:

  • Opening readings and websites. Recovery and church reading rows open external websites (for example the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops, or recovery-literature sites) in an in-app browser. Those websites are operated by third parties under their own privacy policies; visiting them is ordinary web browsing.
  • Finding nearby help (Personal Assistance / Churches). If you use the service-finder and grant location permission, the app sends a geographic query (your approximate area) to public mapping and resource directories — including OpenStreetMap's Overpass API, 211, and FindHelp — to list nearby churches, shelters, food, and support services. These queries are anonymous location lookups; they are not tied to your identity and are not stored by us. You can decline the location permission and search by ZIP code or state instead.
  • The optional AI companion. If you also install the separate MASH app, the "Ask" feature shares the reading you're viewing and your profile with MASH on the same device so it can respond. That exchange happens locally between the two apps and does not leave your phone.

Location

Location permission is optional and used only to find nearby services when you ask. The app does not track your location in the background and does not build a location history.

Children

The app is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from them.

Third-party content and trademarks

The app links to and quotes materials from independent organizations and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by any of them, including Alcoholics Anonymous World Services, Al-Anon Family Group Headquarters, Narcotics Anonymous World Services, or the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops. Trademarks remain the property of their respective owners.

Changes
